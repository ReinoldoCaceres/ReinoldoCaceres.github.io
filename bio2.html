After graduating in 1984 with a degree in education, he began teaching full-time at Duarte H.S. and working on his master's degree. In the mist of teaching, in 1985 he obtained his real estate license with a friend and began working at Century-21 part-time. By 1987, Dennis decided that real estate was where he was meant to be. He went full-time, moving to Southland Properties in Glendora when they first opened that year, and later became a broker.

Dennis has assisted nearly 500 hundred buyers & sellers primarily in the East San Gabriel Valley, with Azusa being his most active and familiar city. In 2011, he took a position at his alma mater, APU, in their Advancement Office, eventually serving as Director of Gift and Estate Planning. In 2022, Dennis stepped away from APU to focus on his role with the City of Azusa, and the decision to run for the same seat in November. He is a member of the Azusa Rotary club and has previously served as a club president, among other leadership roles in church, HOAs and club groups. Dennis and Rhonda bought their first home in 1985 in Azusa and have owned property in the city continuously since. They have lived in their current home in the Rosedale area since 2013.

Passions & Family

Dennis enjoys sports, (still) playing basketball, movies and playing the drums in the worship band at Foothill Community Church in Azusa, where they have worshipped since 1982. His beautiful wife Rhonda, whom he met while in college, is an MFT and she has run Foothill Counseling Center in Azusa for over 25 years. They have 3 adult children who are each married. Brooke (and James - Ceramic Arts) are currently on a yearlong bicycle trip/adventure through Latin America.

She has been a social worker in downtown Seattle, working with at risk youth. Brandon (and Marissa - Nurse), majored in Business/Finance at Azusa Pacific University and now works as a Realtor at Southland Properties in Glendora. Brad (and Kaley - Nurse), also attended APU and was an All-American Discus thrower on their Track team. He is an LA County Firefighter Paramedic. The Beckwith's have 5 joyful grandkids that they are blessed to see often. Dennis has used his recent free time for more family visits and travels to see Brooke and James in Latin America.

Since I arrived in 1980, I have formed a special bond with the city of Azusa. It has become my home. Having attended college here, met my wife here, worked here, worshipped here, owned property here and raised our kids here in the community, my roots have grown deep. I love the history and diversity of our city and have always believed that Azusa is a beautiful city with even greater potential.

I believe we can become a crown jewel of the east San Gabriel Valley

We have abundant resources in people, property, education and commerce.Thanks to the good work of prior counsels and city leaders, we also have a budget surplus that we are already planning to invest in our city infrastructure and community services. We need to further leverage and develop those resources strategically and effectively as we continue to improve and develop our downtown, as well as create safe and enjoyable neighborhoods throughout the city. I want to see Azusa become a desired destination for our neighbors in Southern California, but more importantly a place that all Azusa residents are proud.

There are excellent opportunities to improve our parks and recreation, our public schools, our areas of commerce, our support for the homeless, transportation and much more. We can leverage our future growth with smart and thoughtful development as we provide opportunity for more housing, shopping, restaurants and entertainment options. Working with our faith community and other partners, we can improve the health and wellness of our residences and guests as we partner better together in providing various resources and education opportunities.
